Utkarsh

I am an undergraduate research student at Center for Computational Natural Sciences and Bioinformatics (CCNSB), IIIT Hyderabad, advised by Professor Harjinder Singh. My research interests lie in the applications of Quantum Computing in Natural Sciences, Optimizations, Machine Learning and vice versa.

I am a quantum compute researcher at QpiAI, where I work on developing quantum, quantum-inspired algorithms and a full software stack for their quantum hardware, ASGPTM.

I am also part of IBM Quantum Research Programme and a Qiskit Advocate, IBM. Along with this I'm an active contributor to Qiskit community, and Quantum Open Source Foundation (QOSF) projects such as NISQAI.

In past, I've worked with Professor Apoorva Patel at IISc, Bangalore, and Professor Prasanta K. Panigrahi at IISER Kolkata. The projects majorly focused on simulation, development and application of NISQ Algorithms. I've also worked in an experimental biophysics laboratory under the guidance of Professor Musti J. Swamy at University of Hyderabad.

A Software Simulator for Noisy Quantum Circuits
H. Chaudhary*, B. Mahato*, L. Priyadarshi*, N. Roshan*, Utkarsh* and A. Patel
(Featured in NISQ Newsletter by NASA-USRA, part of India's QuEST initiative)
ArXiv / Talk / Codebase

A software library that simulates noisy quantum logic circuits, in which quantum states are represented by their density matrices, and possible errors in initialisation, logic gates, memory and measurement are implemented using simple models.
